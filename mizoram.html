SANA THONGBA

INGREDIENTS

  • Paneer (I used firm tofu) – 450 grams (chopped into ½ inch pieces)
  • Potato – 1 large (peeled and chopped into 1 inch pieces)
  • Onion – 1 large (Thinly sliced)
  • Green peas – ½ cup
  • Green chilies – 4-5
  • Milk – 1 ½ cup
  • Turmeric powder – 1 tsp
  • Cumin seeds – 1 tsp
  • Oil – 2 – 3 tbsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Cilantro – few for garnish

    • INSTRUCTIONS

  • Cut the paneer into bite size cubes.
  • Heat the oil on a pan and add the bay leaves, cumin seeds. When the seeds splutter,
  • add the sliced onions and sauté for about few minutes. Now add the cubed paneer and then fry in medium flame until it turns light golden.
  • Add ginger or ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies and fry them on low for 30-40 seconds.
  • Now add the potatoes and fry it for about 5 minutes, stirring frequently.
  • Next add the peas and sauté for few more minutes until the peas becomes slightly soft
    • .
  • Add the turmeric powder, chili powder and salt and mix it gently.
  • Now add about 1 to 1 ½ cups of water and cook covered, until the potatoes are soft and completely cooked.
  • Add the milk. Reduce the flame to simmer and let the curry come to a gentle boil. Do not cook on high heat since the milk would curdle.
  • As soon as the curry comes to a gentle boil turn off the flame right away as otherwise the milk would curdle.
  • Garnish the Sana Thongba with cilantro and serve warm with rice or Indian roti.
